# Kolkata Air Quality Analysis (2015-2024)

## Overview
This project analyzes hourly air quality data of Kolkata 
between 2015 and 2024 to identify pollution trends, 
seasonal patterns and key contributing factors to 
air pollution in the city.

## Dataset
- Source: Kaggle
- Duration: 2015 to 2024
- Frequency: Hourly readings
- Pollutants covered: PM2.5, PM10, NO2, CO, SO2, O3
- Additional features: Temperature, Humidity, Wind, Rainfall

## Tools Used
- Python
- Pandas
- Matplotlib
- Google Colab

## Key Findings
- PM2.5 and PM10 levels were lowest in 2020 during 
  the Covid lockdown confirming human activity as the 
  primary pollution driver
- SO2 from industrial sources contributes more to 
  Kolkata's pollution than CO from vehicular emissions
- Pollution peaks at 4am daily due to truck movement, 
  temperature inversion and absence of wind
- Rainfall acts as a natural air purifier — highest 
  pollution spikes occur during dry periods
- O3 levels peak in summer and drop in winter due to 
  temperature driven chemical reactions
- PM2.5 and PM10 show a strong positive correlation 
  of 0.89 suggesting shared pollution sources

## Conclusion
Tackling industrial emissions and regulating early morning 
heavy vehicle movement could be the most effective steps 
toward improving air quality in Kolkata.
